Studying in the Czech Republic: Economics and Finance - Live &amp; Study

May 2024 · 7 minute read

17. 9. 2020 | Fields of Study

Economics is a social science that deals with the production, use and transfer of wealth. The study of economics involves a variety of subject areas such as politics, law, sociology and geography. Obtaining a degree in economics will give you a range of subject-specific skills that will allow you to apply for various jobs as the skills related to economics are sought after by a variety of employers in many fields.

If you are interested in studying Economics in the Czech Republic, take a lot at these Economics programmes taught entirely in English offered by various Czech universities:

Bachelor’s Degree programme in Economics at University of Economics, Prague

The Bachelor of Economics offered by the University of Economics, Prague is a three-year full-time bachelor’s program which is entirely taught in English. The main objective of the program is to create qualified professional economists who will be able to work in both public and private sector worldwide.

Graduates from this programme have a strong base in economics, legal sciences and other social disciplines with a special emphasis on international economics and politics. With a sufficient and varied base, the graduates can respond to the ever-changing and increasingly demanding requirements of business practices and labor markets, both in the Czech Republic and abroad.

Bachelor’s Degree programme in Business Administration with Finance concentration at University of New York in Prague

A three-year study programme in Business Administration with concentration in Finance prepares students for their professional careers by offering several specialized courses in the last two semesters of their study. The courses are taught by skilled and experienced lecturers with proven expertise and credentials in finance and financial services.

The specialized courses include: Risk Management, Hedge Funds and Private Equity, Taxation, Special Topics in Finance, International Money and Banking. Graduates of this programme can work at several positions in both public and private sector, for example as investment manager, financial analyst, bank manager or management consultant.

Bachelor’s Degree programme in Economics and Finance at Charles University

Bachelor in Economics and Finance (BEF) is a three-year program taught fully in English.

The program aims to give the student a thorough grounding in both theoretical and applied economics and finance with an emphasis on developing abstract analytical reasoning. The applicants must have solid background in Mathematics and good command of English.

Courses are taught both by core faculty and professionals from the central bank, commercial banks and business analysts.

Master’s Degree programme in Economics and Finance at Charles University

The two-year Master programme in Economics and Finance taught fully in English offered by the Faculty of Social Sciences at Charles University is designed for students with undergraduate degrees in Business, Finance, Economics, Statistics, Mathematics, Physics, Engineering or related fields.

The program aims to give the student a thorough grounding in both theoretical and applied economics. Courses are taught both by our faculty and professionals from the central bank, commercial banks and business analysts. Graduates of the programme typically assume challenging positions in banking, consulting, investment funds, and public administration.

The aim of this two-year master programme in Economic Data Analysis is to provide students with knowledge and abilities in the area of economic data analysis. The offered courses cover various topics related to data acquisition, data processing, data dissemination and communication, data analysis including advanced statistical and econometric modelling and working with specialized statistical and econometric software as well.

The programme has two specializations: Master in Official Statistics (MOS) and Data Analysis and Modelling (DAM). The students choose their specialization during their first semester.

This two-year master’s programme in Economics and Public Policy taught in English offers students an interactive and personal educational experience. The main aim of the Master of Economics and Public Policy program is to prepare new economists with theoretical and practical knowledge for future positions as economic and financial analysts in both the private and the public sectors, such as in government, the central bank, commercial banks and major international corporations across the world.

Graduates are skilled with theoretical knowledge and advanced analytical skills required to work as a leader in economics, jurisprudence and other social disciplines with a special emphasis on international economics and politics.

Master’s Degree programme in Corporate Strategy and Finance at Charles University

A two-year master’s double-degree programme in Corporate Strategy and Finance based on the Bologna Process aims to facilitate the collaboration between the Institute of Economic Studies, Faculty of Social Sciences, Charles University in Prague and Sciences Po Strasbourg, University of Strasbourg in teaching and academic research in the field of Economics.

The Degree Programme covers two academic years. Each student is requested to spend one academic year at each of the Partner Universities in line with the Curriculum. Successful graduates receive the Diploma Master in “Corporate Strategy and Finance in Europe” from University of Strasbourg and the Master’s degree Diploma with the academic title “Magistr” from Charles University in Prague.

Programme’s graduates will have a broad knowledge and understanding of economic theory with a focus on financial and international economics corresponding to the present state-of-the-art. They can work on positions on the global market, both in the financial sector and multinational companies, as well as in the public sector throughout the European Union.

Master’s Degree programme in Economics at Masaryk University

The two-year programme in Economics offers students the opportunity to obtain in-depth knowledge of industrial organization and transport economics and to master the basics of quantitative tools and techniques used both in economics and in other scientific disciplines. Students of this programme will gain knowledge and skills primarily in microeconomics, macroeconomics, industrial organization, and transport economics. The required courses include Seminar in microeconomics, Transport economics, International economics, Econometrics and Economics of Transition.

Graduates of the Master’s programme in Economics can solve theoretical and applied economic problems, to think analytically, and to use the techniques of theoretical economics with an understanding of the relevance and limits of their use. They are qualified to apply for theoretical and research-based positions in the field of economics and in related disciplines, for example positions in economic journalism and economic analysis for the needs of the public and private sectors.

Master’s Degree programme in Public Finance and Economics at Masaryk University

The two-year Public Finance and Economics study programme is designed for students interested in working in public administration, but also for those who want to work in private, public, or non-governmental non-profit sector. Graduates of this programme understand the relationships between the public and private sectors, as they gain a deep knowledge of the system of public finance and on the effective management of public funds, both on the part of public revenues (tax system, the administration of taxes and fees), and on the side of public expenditures in various spheres.

An essential part of the field is the issue of public policies, especially the evaluation of their redistributive impacts and their impact on the efficiency of the public and private sector. Students in this programme are required to take courses such as Econometrics, Applied Research in Public Policy Making, Public Policy Analysis, Communication and Managerial Skills Training and Public Project Design and Evaluation.

Master’s Degree programme in Finance at Masaryk University

Masaryk University also offers a two-year study programme in Finance. This programme is designed for students who would like to work as experts in various departments in financial institutions, especially in banks, insurance companies, and funds, and in financial and accounting departments of companies and other organizations.

In this programme, students gain knowledge of monetary systems, corporate finance, accounting, financial markets, and international finance. The offered courses include Financial Investments, Managerial Accounting, Auditing, Microeconomics and Taxation.

Graduates of this programme are prepared to work in higher management positions in the financial departments of banks, insurance institutions, and as financial and capital markets analysts, brokers, auditors, portfolio managers, tax consultants, etc.

Doctoral Programme in Economics and Finance at Charles University

The Faculty of Social Sciences at Charles University offers a graduate program in Economics leading to a Ph.D. degree in Economics and Finance. The programme focuses on economic theory and its applications in economic and financial analyses including the modeling and analyses of economic processes and the behavior of economic agents, using modern microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, econometrics and finance. The doctoral program aims to prepare highly qualified and trained experts for academia, international organizations, financial sector as well as for the other industries.

Duties of the programme students primarily consists of research activities, where research papers form the dissertation. Students are also expected to serve as teaching assistants and to actively devote themselves to research through grant proposals and participation at conferences and seminars. A selected number of Ph.D. candidates can participate in the organization of the doctoral program as members of the Center for Doctoral Studies (CDS) and thus become junior researchers with additional financial support.
